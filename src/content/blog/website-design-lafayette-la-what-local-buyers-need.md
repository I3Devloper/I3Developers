When a Lafayette buyer visits your website, they are making a decision in seconds. Not minutes. Seconds.

The difference between a website that generates calls and one that collects digital dust is not how pretty it looks. It is whether it answers the questions a real buyer has before they pick up the phone.

## The Pages Every Lafayette Business Website Needs

### A Clear Service Page

Your homepage is not a service page. Your service page is a service page. Each core service should have its own dedicated page with:

- A headline that states what you do and who you do it for
- A clear explanation of the process
- Proof that you have done this before
- A direct path to contact you

If a visitor has to guess what you do or hunt for a phone number, the page is failing.

### Proof That You Can Deliver

Lafayette buyers are skeptical. They should be. Anyone can claim to be good at something. Your website needs to show it:

- Google reviews embedded on relevant pages
- Before and after examples
- Client names and industries (with permission)
- Specific results, not vague claims

### Speed That Does Not Waste Their Time

A slow website tells a buyer you do not care about their time. Period. Pages should load in under three seconds on mobile. If yours does not, you are losing visitors to competitors who load faster.

### FAQs That Answer Real Questions

The questions your sales team hears every week should be on your website. This saves time for everyone and builds trust with buyers who are researching options.

### Clear Calls to Action

Every page should have a natural next step. Not a screaming banner. A clear, contextual invitation to call, request a quote, or schedule a consultation.

## What Most Lafayette Websites Get Wrong

Most local business websites are built around what the business wants to say, not what the buyer needs to hear. The result is pages full of company history, generic stock photos, and vague promises about quality and service.

Buyers do not care about your mission statement. They care about whether you can solve their problem, whether you have done it before, and how to contact you.

Build your website around those three things and you will outperform most of your competition.
