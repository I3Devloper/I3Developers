If you think local SEO is about stuffing your website with "Lafayette" and your service keyword, you are about five years behind.

Google's local ranking algorithm looks at dozens of signals. The ones that matter most for Lafayette businesses are not what most agencies focus on.

## What Actually Moves the Needle for Local SEO

### Your Google Business Profile

This is the single most important asset for local search. Your profile needs:

- Accurate business name, address, and phone number
- Correct primary and secondary categories
- A detailed business description with relevant keywords
- Regular posts linking to your website content
- Photos updated every few weeks
- Questions and answers populated with real buyer questions

### Review Quality and Velocity

Not just the number of stars. Google looks at:

- How many reviews you get per month
- The recency of your reviews
- Whether reviews mention your services and location
- How you respond to reviews (both positive and negative)

A steady stream of genuine reviews from real customers is one of the strongest local ranking signals you can build.

### Service Page Relevance

Your website needs pages that clearly and thoroughly describe each service you offer, written for the way local buyers actually search. Not for search engines. For humans.

Each service page should answer:

- What is the service?
- Who is it for?
- How does the process work?
- What makes you different?
- How do I get started?

### Local Citations and Consistency

Your business name, address, and phone number need to be consistent across every directory, social profile, and mention online. Inconsistencies confuse Google and weaken your local authority.

### Content That Demonstrates Expertise

Blog posts, case studies, and industry pages that show you understand your market. Not generic content. Specific, local, useful content that a competitor could not write.

## The Mistake Most Lafayette Businesses Make

They hire an agency that focuses entirely on keywords and backlinks while ignoring the Google Business Profile, reviews, and on-page content. Those things matter, but they are not the foundation.

Start with your business profile, your service pages, and your review strategy. Then layer in content and links. That is the order that works.
